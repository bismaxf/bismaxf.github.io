Birria Tacos

Birria tacos are a traditional Mexican dish featuring tender, flavorful meat, typically beef or goat, served in a warm tortilla and accompanied by a rich and savory consommé for dipping.

Preparation Time

  • Total: 4-6 hours
  • Preparation: 30 minutes
  • Marination: 2-4 hours
  • Cooking: 3-4 hours

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck roast, cut into chunks
  • 4 dried guajillo chilies
  • 2 dried ancho chilies
  • 2 dried pasilla chilies
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp dried oreganos
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • Corn tortillas
  • Chopped onions, cilantro, lime wedges, and radishes for serving

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a baking dish or line it with parchment paper.
  2. Marinate Beef: In a bowl, combine diced beef with minced garlic, chopped onion, dried oregano, ground cumin, salt, and pepper. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Cook Beef: Heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add marinated beef and sear until browned. Transfer beef to the prepared baking dish.
  4. Bake: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 2-3 hours until the beef is tender and easily shredded with a fork.
  5. Prepare Tacos: Warm corn tortillas on a skillet or directly over a flame. Fill each tortilla with a portion of the cooked beef.
  6. Serve: Garnish tacos with chopped onion, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice. Serve hot and enjoy the rich flavors of beef birria tacos.
